# Terminology

This file defines the preferred vocabulary for the book.

## Core Terms

### Attention

The listener's perceived focus over time.

### Movement

Any change that keeps a sound alive: modulation, drift, spectral evolution, performance, automation.

### Weight

Perceived mass, warmth, density and solidity. Typically created through harmonic enrichment, saturation, compression and low-mid control.

### Distance

Perceived depth and spatial placement. Typically created through reverb, delay, tone, level and transient softness.

### Memory

The way delay, repetition and echoes cause past musical events to remain present.

### Erosion

The process of breaking, smearing, granulating or weathering a sound until it becomes textural.

### Pressure

Dynamic density, compression, impact and controlled force.

### Drift

Slow instability in pitch, timing, phase, stereo position or timbre.

### Horizon

Stereo scale, width and perceived lateral space.

### Focus

The element or dimension currently holding the listener's attention.
