# Chapter 1 — Attention Mapping

## Why Map Attention?

Waveforms show amplitude.

Spectrograms show frequency.

Neither shows where a listener's attention travels.

Attention Mapping provides a simple way to observe how focus shifts through a piece of music.

## The Attention Timeline

Divide a track into equal time segments.

For each segment record:

- Primary focus
- Secondary focus
- Weight
- Distance
- Movement
- Density
- Emotional note

Patterns quickly emerge.

## Reading a Map

Healthy long-form music rarely keeps attention fixed.

Instead, it rotates attention gradually between layers while preserving continuity.

The goal is not constant novelty.

The goal is sustained curiosity.

## Studio Exercise

Create an Attention Map for one of your own productions.

Then create another for a favourite reference track.

Compare how attention moves rather than how sounds are processed.
