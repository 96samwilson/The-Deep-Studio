# Chapter 1 — Frequency as Perception

## Beyond EQ

Frequency is often taught as a technical subject measured in hertz. While that knowledge is useful, the listener never experiences numbers. They experience emotional balance.

A boost at 200 Hz is not inherently 'correct'. It is only valuable if it changes the listener's perception in the desired direction.

## Four Questions

Before touching an equaliser, ask:

1. Does the sound need more Weight?
2. Does it need more Distance?
3. Is Focus being masked?
4. Is Density increasing or decreasing?

## Emotional Regions

| Region | Typical Perception |
|---|---|
| Low | Stability, gravity, warmth |
| Low-mid | Body, presence |
| Mid | Clarity, intelligibility |
| High-mid | Energy, urgency |
| High | Air, openness |

Treat these as tendencies rather than fixed rules.

## Studio Exercise

Choose a finished mix and make three alternate versions:

- One emphasising Weight.
- One emphasising Focus.
- One emphasising Distance.

Listen the following day without looking at the session and write down only what you perceive.
