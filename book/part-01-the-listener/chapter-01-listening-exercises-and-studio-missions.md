# Chapter 1 — Listening Exercises & Studio Missions

These exercises are intended to train perception rather than technical skill. Repeat them regularly and keep notes in a listening journal.

## Listening Exercise 1 — Follow Your Attention

Choose a track that rewards long listening.

Without looking at the waveform or project, note every moment your attention naturally shifts. Record:
- the timestamp
- what changed
- how it felt

Avoid naming equipment or effects. Describe the experience instead.

## Listening Exercise 2 — One Sound for Ten Minutes

Create a single sustained sound using the Roland JU-06A or another harmonic source.

Keep the harmony constant for ten minutes.

Maintain interest only through:
- movement
- filtering
- saturation
- ambience
- stereo width
- granular texture

## Studio Mission 1 — Print, Don't Tweak

Build one complete processing chain.

Print three versions:
1. Dry
2. Moderate processing
3. Extreme processing

Archive all three. Never overwrite them.

## Studio Mission 2 — Build a Vocabulary

Listen back to your recordings and describe them using only the language introduced in this chapter:

- Weight
- Distance
- Movement
- Focus

Do not describe plug-ins, synthesis methods or processors.

## Reflection

Ask yourself:

- Which sounds held my attention longest?
- Which changes were too obvious?
- Which transformations were almost invisible?
- What would I slow down next time?

These answers become the foundation of your own production philosophy.
